Summary

Adding mathematical rigor to our understanding of neural networks allows us to build more complex and effective models. In this lesson, you learned how to:

  • Calculate the weighted sum \( z \) for neurons using inputs, weights, and biases.
  • Apply activation functions to compute the output \( a \) of neurons.
  • Use vector and matrix notation to simplify computations in layers with multiple neurons.

In the next lesson, we'll delve deeper into matrix and vector notation, further streamlining these calculations and preparing us for more advanced topics.
